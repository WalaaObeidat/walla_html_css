    Hydration is a key to be energized and healthy. Many do not realize the importance of drinking water during hot temperatures. Doctors and health experts encourage keep being hydrated during the summer months. With warmer temperatures,
    a lot of water and electrolytes are depleted with perspiration and we need to replace the water you lose to avoid dehydration and drink enough throughout the day.
    There are some common signs of dehydration: dry or sticky mouth, thirst, dry skin, sleepiness or tiredness,
    decreased urine output, urine is darker than usual, almost no tears when crying, headache, confusion, dizziness.
    Every cell in our body and every process within requires water to function properly. It is about 70% of the water in our body.
    We need water to turn the food into the fuel that keeps us energetic. In summer, drinking water is essential for good health.
    The best way to know you are drinking enough water is by making sure your urine is consistently colorless or light yellow.
